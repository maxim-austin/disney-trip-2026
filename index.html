What you'll experience:
Avatar Flight of Passage Watch POV ▶
You'll climb onto a banshee and soar over the alien world of Pandora — swooping through floating mountains, skimming over water, and feeling the banshee breathe beneath you. The most immersive ride at Disney World.
Expedition Everest Watch POV ▶
A high-speed train ride through the Himalayas — the track breaks ahead of you, the train goes backward in the dark, and you come face-to-face with a giant Yeti. Thrilling but not too scary at 44" height requirement.
Na'vi River Journey
A gentle, glowing boat ride through a bioluminescent rainforest on Pandora. No drops, no speed — just beautiful glowing plants, alien creatures, and a stunning animatronic Na'vi shaman singing at the end. Perfect cool-down after Flight of Passage.
Kilimanjaro Safaris Watch POV ▶
An 18-minute open-air truck ride through a 110-acre African savanna with real giraffes, elephants, lions, zebras, and hippos roaming freely. Go early for active animals, and again at golden hour for the best photo light.
Kali River Rapids
A whitewater raft ride through a rainforest — you will get soaked! The perfect way to cool off in the Florida heat. Bring ponchos or embrace the splash.
Festival of the Lion King
A spectacular 30-minute Broadway-style show with acrobats, fire dancers, and all the Lion King songs. Air-conditioned theater — a perfect mid-morning rest. She'll be singing "Hakuna Matata" for the rest of the trip.

What you'll experience:
Guardians: Cosmic Rewind Watch POV ▶
Disney's first reverse-launch coaster! You blast backward into space, then spin and twist through the galaxy with an awesome classic rock soundtrack. Your seat rotates 360° so you're always facing the action. One of the best rides at Disney World.
Test Track Watch POV ▶
Design your own virtual car, then ride it through a series of tests — heat, cold, sharp turns, and rough roads — before blasting outside onto a banked track at 65 mph, the fastest ride at Walt Disney World!
Frozen Ever After Watch POV ▶
A magical boat ride through Arendelle with Anna, Elsa, Olaf, and Sven — featuring stunning animatronics singing "Let It Go." There's a small backward drop that adds a fun surprise. Located in the Norway pavilion.
Remy's Ratatouille Adventure
You shrink down to the size of a rat and race through Gusteau's restaurant kitchen in a trackless ride vehicle — dodging chef's knives, sizzling pans, and mopping buckets. The 3D effects make it feel like you're really tiny!
Soarin' Around the World
Your feet dangle as you hang-glide over the Eiffel Tower, Great Wall of China, African elephants, and more — with wind, scents, and a massive IMAX screen making it feel absolutely real. A calm, beautiful ride perfect for all ages.
World Showcase
Walk around a 1.2-mile lagoon visiting 11 countries — try treats in Mexico, browse shops in Japan, watch street performers in the UK, and explore miniature landmarks. During the Flower & Garden Festival, there are special topiaries and a Spike scavenger hunt she'll love.
Luminous: The Symphony of Us Watch show ▶
EPCOT's nighttime spectacular over World Showcase Lagoon — fireworks, fountains, lasers, and Disney music celebrating cultures from around the world. Find a spot near the Italy or Japan pavilion for the best views.

What you'll experience:
Rise of the Resistance Watch POV ▶
The best ride at Disney World — period. You're recruited by the Resistance, captured by the First Order (a real Star Destroyer hangar!), and escape in a wild trackless ride with laser blasts, free-fall drops, and AT-ATs. An 18-minute multi-part adventure unlike anything else.
Tower of Terror Watch POV ▶
Step into a haunted 1930s hotel, board a service elevator, and experience random sequences of drops and rises — you never know when the floor will disappear. The spookiest ride at Disney, but the drops are more thrilling than scary. Check if she's up for it!
Slinky Dog Dash Watch POV ▶
A family-friendly roller coaster through Toy Story Land where you ride on Slinky Dog's back — gentle hills, fun turns, and the whole area is designed to make you feel toy-sized in Andy's backyard. She'll love this one.
Millennium Falcon: Smugglers Run Watch POV ▶
She gets to pilot the actual Millennium Falcon! A crew of 6 works together as pilots, gunners, and engineers on a smuggling mission. Request the pilot seat on the left — she'll be steering and jumping to lightspeed.
Toy Story Mania!
A 3D shooting game on a moving ride vehicle — pull the string launcher to hit targets in Toy Story mini-games. Compete for the highest score! Fun for all ages and endlessly replayable.
Mickey & Minnie's Runaway Railway
A trackless dark ride where you get pulled into a Mickey Mouse cartoon — the wacky, colorful scenes change around you as Goofy's train goes off the rails. No height requirement and full of surprises.
Galaxy's Edge
An entire Star Wars planet brought to life — the full-size Millennium Falcon sits outside, stormtroopers patrol the streets, and you can build a custom lightsaber ($250) or a droid ($100). Just walking around feels like being in the movies.
Fantasmic! Watch show ▶
A 30-minute nighttime spectacular where Mickey battles Disney villains using water screens, fireworks, lasers, and a 40-foot fire-breathing dragon. The perfect way to end the day — arrive 30 min early for good seats.

What you'll experience:
TRON Lightcycle / Run Watch POV ▶
You lean forward onto a lightcycle motorcycle and launch from 0 to 60 mph into a neon-lit digital world. The fastest coaster at Magic Kingdom and absolutely stunning at night — ride it once with your LLSP, then re-ride after fireworks when the wait drops to 15 min.
Seven Dwarfs Mine Train Watch POV ▶
A swaying mine cart coaster through the diamond mine from Snow White — the cars rock side-to-side on curves, and inside you'll see the dwarfs singing "Heigh-Ho" with incredible animatronics. Gentle enough for her, thrilling enough for you.
Space Mountain Watch POV ▶
A classic indoor roller coaster in near-complete darkness — you rocket through space with stars zooming past. It feels faster than it is because you can't see what's coming. A rite of passage for every Disney kid!
Big Thunder Mountain Watch POV ▶
The "wildest ride in the wilderness" — a runaway mine train that zooms through canyons, caves, and past a flooded town. Fast turns and bunny hops but no big drops, so it's thrilling without being scary. Reopening May 3 with new magic!
Tiana's Bayou Adventure Watch POV ▶
Join Princess Tiana on a musical boat ride through the bayou to find the perfect ingredient for a big party — featuring incredible animatronics, New Orleans jazz, and a 50-foot splashdown finale. You will get a little wet!
Haunted Mansion Watch POV ▶
Glide through a mansion filled with 999 happy haunts — dancing ghosts in the ballroom, a singing graveyard, and a ghost that hitches a ride in your Doom Buggy. More fun-spooky than scary. A must-ride classic.
Pirates of the Caribbean
A gentle boat ride through pirate-infested waters — cannonball battles, treasure caves, and Captain Jack Sparrow hiding around every corner. Two small drops at the start add a splash of excitement. A Disney original since 1973.
Peter Pan's Flight
Your pirate ship flies over a miniature London and Neverland — one of the most magical dark rides at Disney. Lines are always long, so hitting it right after rope drop is the move.
It's a Small World
The most iconic Disney ride — a peaceful boat float past hundreds of singing dolls representing cultures from around the world. The song will be stuck in your head forever, and she'll love every colorful room.
Festival of Fantasy Parade
A dazzling afternoon parade down Main Street with massive floats, beloved characters, dancers, and music. Rapunzel's tower, Maleficent as a fire-breathing dragon, and all the princesses. Grab a spot 20 min early near the castle.
Happily Ever After Watch show ▶
The most magical 18 minutes at Disney — fireworks, projections on Cinderella Castle, lasers, and music from every Disney classic. Stand on Main Street U.S.A. for the full experience. This is the moment that makes the whole trip.

Pro tips
Things first-timers forget
Schedule every morning Uber the night before
Flamingo Crossings has fewer available drivers than central Disney. Scheduled rides are far more reliable than on-demand morning requests.
Free ice water at any counter
Just ask at any quick-service restaurant. Disney charges $3.50+ for bottled water. Stay aggressively hydrated — May heat is 87–90°F.
Mobile order everything
Place food orders through the My Disney Experience app 30–60 minutes before you want to eat. Saves 15–30 minutes of line standing.
The 9:45 PM TRON re-ride
After Happily Ever After fireworks, half the park leaves. TRON waits drop from 60+ to 15 minutes. Ride it again in the dark with neon blazing. This is the moment she'll remember forever.
Midday breaks are non-negotiable
On AK and HS days, Uber back to the hotel for pool time between noon and 2:30 PM. On EPCOT and MK days, rest inside air-conditioned attractions. Prevents meltdowns and heat exhaustion.
Magic Kingdom Uber warning
MK has no rideshare pickup. You must monorail or ferry to the Transportation & Ticket Center (~15 min), then Uber from TTC. Budget extra time when leaving. Wait 15 min after fireworks to avoid surge pricing.
